Why Forex & Indices?

I'm drawn to forex and indices trading for several compelling reasons:

  • Market Efficiency: The 24-hour nature of forex markets and the liquidity of major indices provide countless opportunities to apply systematic strategies across multiple timeframes.
  • Institutional Participation: These markets are heavily influenced by smart money, making them ideal for order flow analysis and understanding how large players position themselves.
  • Leverage & Accessibility: The ability to control significant positions with proper risk management allows for capital efficiency while maintaining strict 1% risk per trade discipline.
  • Pure Technical Analysis: Price action in these markets reflects real-time sentiment shifts, economic data releases, and institutional positioning, creating a rich environment for technical traders.

My Trading Philosophy

"Amateurs think about how much money they can make. Professionals think about how much money they could lose." – Jack Schwager

This quote fundamentally shaped how I approach every trade. Before I consider potential profits, I ask myself: "What's my maximum loss, and can I survive it?" This mindset shift transformed my trading from gambling into a sustainable profession.

My philosophy is built on three defensive principles:

  • Capital Preservation Above All: Never risk more than 1% per trade. Every trade is executed knowing exactly what I'm willing to lose before thinking about what I could gain. My first job is to protect my account, not grow it aggressively.
  • Position Sizing = Risk Control: I size positions based on stop-loss distance, not on how confident I feel. Emotions don't determine risk,mathematics does. This ensures that even a string of losses won't devastate my account.
  • Worst-Case Scenario Planning: Before entering any trade, I visualize the worst possible outcome. If price stops me out, will I be able to trade tomorrow? If the answer is no, I don't take the trade. Survival first, profits second.

By focusing obsessively on what I could lose rather than what I could make, I've found that profits take care of themselves. A 1:4 risk-reward ratio means I only need to be right 25% of the time to break even—but my edge comes from being right 40-50% of the time while never risking more than I can afford to lose.
